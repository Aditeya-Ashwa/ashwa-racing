01Role & Responsibility

The Brakes & Logistics subsystem ensures the vehicle stops reliably and that parts, tools and test logistics are handled efficiently. We design, test and maintain braking assemblies — calipers, discs, pads, hydraulics — while managing spares, workshops, transport, and race-day logistics.

Safety, repeatability and thermal performance are the primary engineering goals. The subsystem collaborates closely with Chassis, Electronics and Driverless teams to ensure braking integration and reliable data capture during development and racing.

02Core Areas

01

Mechanical Braking Design

Design and validate calipers, discs, mounting interfaces and brake cooling paths using CAD and simulation.

02

Hydraulics & Control

Select and tune master cylinders, proportioning valves and ABS interfaces, integrating brake-by-wire signals where applicable.

03

Testing & Validation

Conduct dynamometer, bench and on-track tests. Measure deceleration, pad wear, rotor temps and fade behaviour.

04

Logistics & Workshop

Manage spare inventories, toolkits, transport logistics, workshop scheduling and race-day setups.

03Vision & Roadmap

We aim to build braking systems that are predictable under load, light yet robust, supported by efficient logistics processes. Our roadmap emphasizes thermal management, tighter integration with control systems and streamlined spare-parts flow.
